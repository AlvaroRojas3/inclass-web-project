Welcome To My Personal Profile!

By Alvaro I. Rojas

September 8, 2024

Introduction

Hello, my name is Alvaro Rojas but my friends call me Al.
I'm a 25 year old Chilean/American attending my 2nd semester here at Weber State University as a Computer Science student.
I'm an Air Force veteran who recently completed his 4 year contract as a Medic and Instructor.
I made an important decision to join in 2020 as I was not sure exactly what I wanted to do after high school and wanted to leave my small town in Idaho Falls, Idaho.
Once I signed my contract and completed Basic Military Traning "aka BMT", I was selected to become a trained Dental Laboratory Technician.
However, once the Covid - 19 epademic began, I was later re-trained as an emergency combat medic later that year.
Most of my adult life was spent under intense training, long hours, and countless trails.
On the other hand, however, I also got to expirience a lot of the world, met amazing mentors alongside friends, and have become confident in my ability to tackle difficult things.
I now begin my journey to become a computer scientist and aspire to work in the software engineering world!

Awards

During my time in military service, I have had many opportunities to shine.
One of my proudest accomplished awards was when I recieved my TCCC (Tactical Combat Casualty Care) Award and Instructor Certification.
This award is given to individuals who complete a tough mental/physical team course that puts them in simulated combat scenarios while learning how to apply life-saving measures to critically injured soldiers.
This specific life-saving course quickly became a required course in the Air Force due to its effectiveness and deployment capabilities.
So much so that I was selected to become an instructor, alongside other highly accomplished Airmen, to train up to 1,200 individuals who were set for deployment in 2024.
The task took 9 months but after all the traning was completed, I personally recieved acknowledgement from our base commander and was awarded Airman of the Year in 2023.
A title that only the people who go above and beyond mission standards get to receive.
As it still hangs proudly in my office, I continue to live life in a way that resembles recieving such an award. Never-ending hunger for improvement.

Hobbies

However there are some hobbies that I enjoy doing all year long. Some of these hobbies include:

Working out is pretty self-explanatory as my health has always had to be in top shape to progress within the military ranks.

It allows you to get into a squad with your friends and battle it out using a variety of different weapons and skill sets.

Learning-wise, I have always enjoyed reading a lot of sci-fi and fantasy books.
My love for reading action-packed world-building books became suprisingly helpful.
Especially when I started my educational journey at Weber State University.
My vocabulary was already on a higher level and it was a lot easier to grasp more complicated concepts because of all the reading that I had done in my younger years.
Now whenever I'm able, I look for ways to not only improve just my academics but also every aspect of my life.
